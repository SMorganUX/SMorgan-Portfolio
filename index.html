HOBBIES

Reading

I've always been an avid reader and while the extent of my reading has declined in busier adult life, I still try to make an effort to read regularly. In recent years, I've used audiobooks as a way to accomplish that by listening to them whenever I work on projects or housework and have expanded my library to include more studious material than just my usual fiction.

Exercise

Being prior military, working out at least semi-regularly has been a part of my weekly schedule, even if I'm not quite as devoted to it as I was when it was a part of my job. I find it's important to at least get a healthy amount of exercise in as someone who spends so much time at a desk.

Digital Art

I get the same kind of satisfaction to drawing as I do with the design process of UI or graphic design. I enjoy making things look nice and experimenting with different design. It was the prerequisite to me learning how to code so that I could do this with webpages and applications as well.

Cooking

I don't actually enjoy cooking. I just like eating new and different foods, so I learned to cook.

Videogames

While not the most productive of hobbies like reading or exercise, I enjoy occasionally playing games that tickle that same creative itch as reading. Games with puzzles to make my brain work or ones that help keep my motorskills in good form tend to be my favorites, even if the latter have been more a training in patience and perseverence than skill. I get a kick out of games that allow me to get more technical, making spreadsheets of data for the sake of min-maxing some aspect of the game.
